# Slidebox35

## Overview

This is a lightbox and frame for displaying up to 4 35mm slides. The frame is designed to fit
[this 10" light](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0FWQZH4X7) (around $20 for two; I recommend the
3-color version).

The light can be set to be motion-sensitive or turned on/off directly. It is rechargeable
with a USB-c connector. In motion-sensitive mode, a charge can last a long time, unless you
have cats that constantly walk by it. The light is also magnetic, and the magnets are strong 
enough to hold the light and a Slidebox35 frame on most magnetic surfaces.

## Versions and printing

There are several versions of the Slidebox35. One distinction is whether it fits 1mm or 2mm 
slides. Standard slide mounts that you might get from a lab are about 1mm and will fit that 
version. However, it's hard to print your own slide mounts that thin; the 2mm frame will 
fit 2mm-thick 3d printed slide mounts.

The frame should be printed vertically, as found in the STLs. Each file also includes a small
cap that can be slid in after the slides to hold them in place. The 2mm frame also includes
a shim for using with 1mm slides.

**slidebox35-magnetic-1mm.stl** has no back, is designed to use the magnet, and fits 1mm slides.

**slidebox35-magnetic-2mm.stl** has no back, uses the magnet, and fits 2mm slides.

**slidebox35-wallmount.stl** has a back with multiple holes for hanging on the wall.

**slidebox35-tabletop-leg.stl** is a small stand that will hold up the magnetic frame
on a tabletop surface. It does not fit the wallmount version.
